Our Story

It started with a single solar-powered bug zapper.

I asked Grok whether trygroundguard.com was legitimate. One conversation path recommended it as the “top pick.” Another correctly flagged it as a scam funnel selling an overpriced generic product.

That contradiction — plus the fact that I had personally been burned by a similar humidifier scam months earlier — made it painfully clear: the internet is now flooded with sophisticated fake review sites designed to trick shoppers.

So we built ReviewGuard: a dead-simple tool that searches only trusted expert review sites (Wirecutter, RTINGS, The Spruce, CNET, and others) and completely ignores the scam ecosystem.

This tool exists because I got tired of being lied to when trying to buy something good for my family.
